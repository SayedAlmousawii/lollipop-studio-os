# Studio OS — UX-03 Adjustment Workspace Planning Document

# Purpose

This document defines the proposed “Adjustment Workspace” workflow for Studio OS.

The goal is to solve the operational friction introduced by the current locked-invoice adjustment architecture while preserving immutable accounting behavior and audit safety.

This is primarily:
- a workflow/UX improvement
- an operational abstraction layer
- a staged financial editing system

It is NOT intended to redesign the underlying accounting architecture.

---

# Background

Studio OS currently uses immutable locked invoices.

Once a final invoice is locked:
- the original invoice must never be modified
- future financial changes create adjustment invoices instead

This behavior was introduced as part of the financial hardening roadmap and is architecturally correct.

However, real-world operational testing revealed significant UX and workflow problems after introducing post-lock adjustment behavior.

---

# Current Behavior (Problem)

Current flow:

1. Final invoice becomes locked
2. Staff adds/removes package items or add-ons
3. System immediately creates real adjustment invoices
4. Every subsequent change creates additional adjustment invoices
5. Removals immediately trigger manager approval flows
6. Accounting concepts become exposed directly in operational workflows

Examples:
- add album
- remove album
- change album size
- add prints
- remove prints
- upgrade package
- downgrade package

Each action currently becomes an immediate financial event.

---

# Why This Is Problematic

## 1. Real Customer Interactions Are Iterative

Customers frequently:
- change their mind
- compare options
- experiment with upgrades
- add/remove items multiple times during one discussion

Operational staff need flexibility during this process.

The current system treats temporary editing behavior as finalized accounting behavior.

---

## 2. Adjustment Invoice Explosion

Current behavior can generate:

- ADJ-00003
- ADJ-00004
- ADJ-00005
- ADJ-00006

during a single customer conversation.

This creates:
- accounting clutter
- operational confusion
- audit noise
- document overload
- poor UX

---

## 3. Manager Approval Happens Too Early

Current behavior:
- removing an item immediately triggers manager approval

But operationally:
- staff may still be experimenting
- customer may change their mind moments later
- final net financial impact may end up positive anyway

Manager approval should govern finalized reductions, not temporary editing actions.

---

## 4. Operational Users Are Exposed To Accounting Internals

Operational staff care about:
- what the customer currently owns
- what the customer currently owes
- what still needs payment

They do NOT naturally think in:
- immutable invoice snapshots
- adjustment chains
- credit-note issuance
- document lineage

The current workflow exposes too much accounting complexity directly into operational editing.

---

# Proposed Solution — Adjustment Workspace

## Core Concept

After a final invoice is locked:
- the normal POS/order editing interface becomes locked/read-only
- staff must explicitly enter an “Adjustment Workspace”

The Adjustment Workspace acts as:
- a temporary editable staging area
- a draft post-lock modification session
- an operational editing layer before accounting finalization

---

# High-Level Workflow

## Step 1 — Final Invoice Locked

Original invoice remains immutable.

Normal invoice editing disabled.

Staff sees:
- locked financial state
- operational order summary
- button:
  > Open Adjustment Workspace

---

## Step 2 — Open Adjustment Workspace

System creates:
- temporary adjustment session
OR
- adjustment draft state

NOT yet:
- finalized adjustment invoice
- finalized accounting document

The workspace allows:
- additions
- removals
- upgrades
- downgrades
- quantity changes
- package modifications

freely during the editing session.

---

## Step 3 — Staff Performs Edits

Staff can:
- add/remove/change items repeatedly
- compare pricing with customer
- iterate safely

UI should clearly show:
- original locked composition
- pending additions
- pending removals
- resulting balance difference

No finalized accounting documents are created yet.

---

## Step 4 — Finalize Workspace

Possible actions:
- Issue Adjustment
- Record Payment
- Save Draft
- Cancel Workspace

At finalization:
- system calculates net financial difference
- system creates ONE consolidated adjustment result

Possible outputs:
- positive adjustment invoice
- negative adjustment / credit note
- mixed adjustment result
- no-op if no changes remain

---

# Manager Approval Rules

Manager approval should occur ONLY at finalization stage.

Suggested rule:
- if finalized net result reduces customer/order value:
  - require manager approval
- otherwise:
  - no approval required

This avoids:
- approval spam
- operational interruption
- unnecessary manager involvement during temporary editing

---

# Important Architectural Principle

The Adjustment Workspace is NOT:
- a real invoice
- a posted accounting document
- immutable financial history

It is:
- a temporary operational editing state

Only finalization produces immutable accounting records.

This preserves:
- financial immutability
- auditability
- accounting correctness

while improving operational UX significantly.

---

# Relationship To Existing Architecture

This proposal does NOT replace:
- locked invoices
- adjustment invoices
- credit notes
- immutable financial history

Instead it adds:
- workflow staging
- operational editing abstraction
- deferred accounting issuance

This should layer naturally on top of the existing architecture.

---

# Operational UX Goals

The Adjustment Workspace should:
- reduce employee friction
- reduce manager interruptions
- reduce adjustment-document clutter
- improve customer interaction flow
- hide accounting complexity from operational staff
- maintain accounting integrity underneath

---

# Suggested UI Concepts

Possible UI ideas:
- “Open Adjustment Workspace” button
- adjustment draft banner
- pending additions/removals section
- live balance difference preview
- finalize adjustment summary
- warning only on final reduction
- single consolidated adjustment result

---

# Important Distinction

The system now has TWO realities:

## Operational Reality
“What the customer currently has.”

vs

## Accounting Reality
“How the financial history was recorded.”

The Adjustment Workspace primarily exists to improve the operational layer without compromising the accounting layer.

---

# Expected Benefits

## Operational
- smoother customer interactions
- easier editing workflow
- fewer interruptions
- less confusion

## Financial
- fewer micro-adjustment documents
- cleaner audit trails
- cleaner reporting
- reduced accounting noise

## UX
- clearer mental model
- less financial complexity exposed
- more intuitive post-lock behavior

---

# Important Future Compatibility

This concept becomes even more important before implementing:
- gift vouchers
- customer credit
- store credit
- refunds
- partial forfeits
- future wallet systems

Without a staging/editing layer, those systems may significantly increase operational and accounting complexity.

---

# Non-Goals

This proposal is NOT attempting to:
- redesign the accounting subsystem
- remove immutable invoice behavior
- merge invoices together
- remove adjustment invoices entirely
- simplify audit history

The goal is operational workflow improvement while preserving financial correctness.

# Final Summary

The Adjustment Workspace introduces a missing middle layer between:

> “editing the customer’s order”

and

> “creating immutable accounting records.”

This separation is necessary because the current architecture correctly protects accounting history but currently exposes too much financial rigidity directly into normal operational editing workflows.
